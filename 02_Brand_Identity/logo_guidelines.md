# 4. Primary Wordmark Guidelines (UPGRADED)

## **Purpose**
The official, most prestigious representation of the brand.

## **Emotional Tone**
- elegant  
- mature  
- feminine  
- luxurious  
- confident  

## **Symbolic Architecture**
- The “M” subtly references a crown silhouette  
- The “Q” symbolizes feminine identity and inherent value  
- The spacing reflects luxury minimalism  
- The curves reflect softness and emotional warmth  

## **Technical Rules**
- Clear space = height of the “Q”  
- Never compress horizontally  
- Never stretch vertically  
- Never apply drop shadows  
- Never place on cold backgrounds  

## **Approved Colors**
- Warm Ivory  
- Champagne Gold  
- Soft Cocoa  
- Rosé Beige  

## **Usage**
- website headers  
- packaging  
- campaigns  
- editorial layouts  
- product branding  
- luxury collaborations

# 10. Clear Space Requirements (UPGRADED)

Clear space protects the logo’s luxury presence.

### **Primary Wordmark**
Clear space = height of the “Q”

### **Secondary Wordmark**
Clear space = width of the “M”

### **Monogram**
Clear space = full monogram height

### **Crown Symbol**
Clear space = width of one crown point

### **Emblem Lockup**
Clear space = full emblem height

Nothing may enter this space — no text, no imagery, no UI elements.

# 12. Color Usage Rules (UPGRADED)

### **Approved Colors**
- Warm Ivory  
- Champagne Gold  
- Pearl Gold  
- Soft Cocoa  
- Blush Pink (rare)  

### **Never Use**
- pure black (#000000)  
- pure white (#FFFFFF)  
- neon colors  
- cold greys  
- saturated primaries  
- flat yellow “fake gold”  

### **Metallic Rules**
- Gold must be metallic or foil in print  
- Digital gold must use subtle gradients  
- No flat gold in digital UI  

---

# 13. Background Rules (UPGRADED)

### **Allowed Backgrounds**
- warm neutrals  
- blush tones  
- soft ivory  
- champagne beige  

### **Avoid**
- dark backgrounds  
- cold whites  
- high‑contrast patterns  
- busy textures  

### **Never**
- place logo over photography without a soft overlay  
- place logo over saturated colors  
- place logo over harsh shadows  

---

# 14. Logo Misuse (STRICTLY PROHIBITED)

The following are **non‑negotiable violations**:

- stretching or distorting  
- rotating  
- adding shadows or outlines  
- using unapproved colors  
- placing on busy backgrounds  
- adding gradients to the wordmark  
- using harsh black  
- using cold white  
- crowding the logo  
- altering spacing  
- adding decorative elements  
- using outdated crown clichés  
- placing inside shapes (circles, boxes)  
- using low‑resolution versions  
- applying filters  
- warping or bending  
- adding textures  

Any misuse damages brand equity and breaks luxury consistency.
