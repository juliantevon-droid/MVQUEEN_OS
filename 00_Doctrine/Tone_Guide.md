# MVQUEEN — Tone Guide

## The Governing Principle

MVQUEEN speaks like someone who has already arrived. Not loudly. Not urgently. With the ease of a woman who has nothing to prove and everything to offer.

The tone is warm authority — confident enough to not need validation, warm enough to never feel cold.

Miss. Princess speaks like a best friend who genuinely wants you to feel good. Encouraging, soft, never preachy.

## MVQUEEN Tone Profile

MVQUEEN Is: Quiet confidence, genuine warmth, restrained sophistication, earned authority, intentional femininity.
MVQUEEN Is Not: Aggressive, boastful, performative, cold, distant, decorative, weak.

## Miss. Princess Tone Profile

Miss. Princess Is: Warm and light energy, genuine encouragement, intentional playfulness, inviting accessibility, celebratory femininity.
Miss. Princess Is Not: Hyper, pressured, generic, hollow, childish, dumbed-down, performative.

## The Four Tone Rules

### Rule 1 — Say it once
MVQUEEN copy never repeats itself for emphasis. If something needs to be said twice to land, the first version wasn't strong enough. Revise.

### Rule 2 — Earn the adjective
Every adjective must pull its weight. "Beautiful" means nothing. "Velvet-soft" means something. Earn it or cut it.

### Rule 3 — She already is
Language never implies she is incomplete. The product serves her — it does not fix her.

### Rule 4 — No filler sentences
Every sentence advances the story, earns the sale, or builds brand warmth. If it does none — cut it.

## Banned Phrases

- "Elevate your..." — overused, vague
- "Game-changer" — cliché
- "You deserve..." — manipulative trigger
- "Finally..." — implies she was lacking
- "Transform your..." — implies she needs transformation
- "Unleash your..." — aggressive
- "Luxury at an affordable price" — condescending
- "Emotionally" as adverb — banned in copy
- "Ecosystem" in customer-facing copy — internal only
- "Elevated" as standalone adjective — earn it or replace it

## Style Rules

MVQUEEN: Em dashes for rhythm. No exclamation marks. Fragments allowed when intentional. Italics sparingly.
Miss. Princess: Light use of · and ✦. One exclamation mark per caption max. Emoji 1–2 max. Contractions always.
