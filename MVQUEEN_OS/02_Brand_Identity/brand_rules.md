# 📐 MVQUEEN — Brand Rules

---

## What Brand Rules Are

Brand rules are not creative restrictions.
They are the agreements that make MVQUEEN recognizable across every surface, every channel, every collaborator, and every AI output — without requiring someone to approve every single decision.

When the rules are internalized, every output is already brand-aligned before review.
That is the goal.

---

## RULE SET 01 — Identity Rules

**Rule 1.1 — The brand name is always written MVQUEEN.**
Not MvQueen. Not Mv Queen. Not mvqueen (in running copy). Not MV QUEEN.
The only exception: all-lowercase in URL structures and hashtags.

**Rule 1.2 — The sister brand is always written Miss.Princess.**
Not Miss Princess. Not missprincess. Not Miss.princess.
The period between Miss and Princess is structural — it is part of the name.

**Rule 1.3 — The two brands are always treated as distinct.**
They share a world. They do not share a voice, an aesthetic, or a customer.
MVQUEEN: mature, elevated, emotionally sophisticated.
Miss.Princess: youthful, playful, dreamy.
Content for one is never used for the other without intentional adaptation.

**Rule 1.4 — "Most Valuable Queen" is the brand meaning — use it intentionally.**
It is not a tagline to be deployed casually.
It is the philosophical foundation — referenced in brand storytelling, not product copy.

## RULE SET 02 — Visual Identity Rules

**Rule 2.1 — The primary background is always warm, never pure white.**
Warm Ivory (#FAF6F1) or Champagne Neutral (#E8DDD0) on all brand surfaces.
Pure white (#FFFFFF) is never used as a brand background — it breaks the warmth.

**Rule 2.2 — Pure black (#000000) is never used for body text.**
Use Soft Cocoa (#7D5A52 or nearest warm dark equivalent) for all text.
Black creates harshness. MVQUEEN communicates through warmth.

**Rule 2.3 — Gold is an accent, never a base.**
Champagne Gold or Pearl Gold appears in small applications only: logo details, packaging embossing, UI hover states.
Never use gold as a background, primary color block, or dominant visual element.
Gold used too broadly loses its luxury signal.

**Rule 2.4 — Typography follows the dual-family system exclusively.**
Serif (Cormorant Garamond / Playfair Display) for headlines, emotional statements, product names.
Sans-serif (Jost / DM Sans / Raleway) for body copy, UI, labels.
No third font families without explicit brand approval.

**Rule 2.5 — Photography is always warm-lit.**
No cool-toned, blue-filtered, or grey-graded imagery in brand contexts.
Skin tones are never corrected toward cool.
Every image carries warmth in its light — this is non-negotiable.

## RULE SET 03 — Copy & Voice Rules

**Rule 3.1 — Every piece of copy centers the woman, not the brand.**
MVQUEEN copy is written from her perspective, about her experience.
The brand is the setting. She is the protagonist.

**Rule 3.2 — No exclamation marks in body copy. Ever.**
Enthusiasm lives in word choice. Not punctuation.
Exception: SMS only, maximum one per message.

**Rule 3.3 — One CTA per execution.**
One email = one CTA. One ad = one CTA. One caption = one action.
Multiple CTAs fracture attention. Choose one. Trust it.

**Rule 3.4 — The forbidden words list is law.**
`06_Tone_And_Voice/Forbidden_Words.md` is not a suggestion.
Every piece of content is checked against it before publishing.

**Rule 3.5 — AI copy always receives a human pass.**
No AI-generated content publishes without human review.
Review against the voice standard. Refine until it is indistinguishable from purposefully written MVQUEEN copy.

---

## RULE SET 04 — Content Rules

**Rule 4.1 — Quality over frequency on every channel.**
MVQUEEN does not post to fill a calendar.
Every post, email, and piece of content earns its place.
One strong piece outperforms ten mediocre ones.

**Rule 4.2 — 70/30 content ratio.**
70% non-promotional (world-building, identity, education, community, atmosphere).
30% product and purchase-led.
A luxury brand earns the right to sell by first giving her a world to belong to.

**Rule 4.3 — Trends are only adopted when they align with brand doctrine.**
MVQUEEN never chases trends for reach.
A trend that fits is amplified. A trend that doesn't is ignored entirely.
The filter: does this feel like MVQUEEN, or does it feel like we are trying to be something else?

**Rule 4.4 — Every campaign connects to at least one of the five messaging pillars.**
`06_Tone_And_Voice/Messaging_Pillars.md` defines the five pillars.
Off-pillar content is off-brand content.

---

## RULE SET 05 — Operational Rules

**Rule 5.1 — Brand Bible is the final authority.**
When any brand decision is ambiguous — the Brand Bible resolves it.
No personal preference overrides documented brand standards.

**Rule 5.2 — The OS is built to be used, not archived.**
Files are working documents. They are referenced before every relevant task.
A system no one uses is not a system. It is a filing cabinet.

**Rule 5.3 — Wrong content is never published.**
If a file still contains Brand Bible excerpts instead of its intended content — it is treated as empty.
It does not generate outputs until it is properly rebuilt.

**Rule 5.4 — Both brands have separate but connected governance.**
MVQUEEN standards apply to MVQUEEN. Miss.Princess standards apply to Miss.Princess.
Cross-brand contamination — using MVQUEEN voice for Miss.Princess content or vice versa — is a brand error.

---

## The Single Most Important Rule

**Everything MVQUEEN produces must make her feel something — and that feeling must be one she wants to feel again.**

If a piece of content, a product, an email, a package, or a customer interaction does not deliver an emotional experience — it does not meet the standard.

Every rule above exists to protect and enable that single outcome.
