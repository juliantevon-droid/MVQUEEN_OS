# 👑 MVQUEEN — Collection Structure
### The Architecture of the Catalog

---

## What This File Is

A collection is not a product category. It is a world within the world.

This file defines how MVQUEEN builds, organizes, names, launches, and retires collections — across both MVQUEEN and Miss.Princess. It is the structural blueprint for how the catalog grows with intention rather than accumulation.

---

## THE COLLECTION PHILOSOPHY

MVQUEEN does not build a store. It builds worlds.

Each collection is a distinct emotional experience — a world with its own mood, its own palette expression, its own campaign, its own story. Products live inside collections the way rooms live inside a house. Each room is distinct. The house is coherent.

The customer should be able to step into a collection and feel its specific atmosphere — and then recognize that atmosphere as unmistakably MVQUEEN.

---

## PART 1 — COLLECTION TYPES

### Type 01 — The Core Collection (Evergreen)
**What it is:** The permanent backbone of the catalog. Products that never rotate out.
**Emotional identity:** This is the MVQUEEN world at its most essential — the pieces and products she reaches for every day.
**Naming:** Simple, iconic, permanent. *The MVQUEEN Essentials / The Foundation Edit / Core.*
**Products:** Hero skincare, signature fragrances, everyday fashion staples, core beauty.
**Marketing:** Always on. Featured in brand introduction, new customer journeys, and every "start here" touchpoint.

---

### Type 02 — The Seasonal Collection
**What it is:** A thematic collection released in alignment with seasonal emotional shifts — not trend cycles.
**Emotional identity:** Each season carries a distinct feeling. MVQUEEN captures that feeling in a collection.
**Timing:**
- Spring: *softening, renewal, becoming* — February/March launch
- Summer: *warmth, confidence, slow living* — May/June launch
- Autumn: *depth, ritual, returning inward* — August/September launch
- Winter: *intimacy, comfort, quiet luxury* — November launch
**Naming:** Atmospheric, evocative. *Velvet Season / Still Summer / The Return / Ivory Winter.*
**Products:** Mix of existing core products styled into the seasonal story + 2–5 new seasonal products.
**Marketing:** Full campaign. 4–6 week lifecycle.

---

### Type 03 — The Drop Collection
**What it is:** A limited, tightly curated release of 3–8 products. High anticipation, short availability.
**Emotional identity:** Exclusivity without elitism — available to everyone, but not forever.
**Timing:** 4–6 per year, minimum 6 weeks apart.
**Naming:** Statement-level. *Soft Reign / The Arrival / Already Decided.*
**Products:** New products only — no repackaged core items.
**Marketing:** Teaser → early access for existing customers → public launch → sell-through. No restocks announced at launch.
**Availability:** 4–8 weeks or until sold through, whichever comes first.

---

### Type 04 — The Ritual Set (Curated Bundle)
**What it is:** Intentionally curated bundles built around a specific ritual or use-case.
**Emotional identity:** The easiest way to step into a complete MVQUEEN experience.
**Examples:**
- *The Morning Ritual* — cleanser + serum + moisturizer + fragrance mist
- *The Soft Skin Edit* — exfoliator + mask + face oil
- *The Glow Set* — 3 products that build visible luminosity
- *The Gift Edit* — curated for gifting, premium packaging
**Naming:** Ritual-forward. *The [Ritual Name] Set / The [Result] Edit / The [Occasion] Collection.*
**Pricing:** Bundle pricing that offers value over individual purchase (see Pricing_Strategy.md).
**Marketing:** Featured during gifting seasons, new customer campaigns, and "how to start" content.

---

### Type 05 — The Collaboration / Limited Edition
**What it is:** A special collection built in partnership with a creator, artist, or cultural moment.
**Emotional identity:** MVQUEEN's world filtered through another distinct creative lens.
**Timing:** Maximum 2 per year. Strategic, not reactive.
**Naming:** Includes both MVQUEEN and collaborator identity: *MVQUEEN × [Name] — [Collection Name]*
**Products:** Exclusively new. No core products repackaged with a new label.
**Marketing:** Full campaign. Collaborator audience + MVQUEEN audience. Pre-launch waitlist required.

---

### Type 06 — The Miss.Princess Collection
**What it is:** Collections under the Miss.Princess brand identity — distinct from MVQUEEN collections.
**Emotional identity:** Softer, more playful, more trend-responsive — but same quality standard.
**Naming:** Carries Miss.Princess energy: *The Pink Edit / Soft Girl Season / Princess Era / Petal Drop.*
**Products:** Can include cross-category bundles unique to Miss.Princess.
**Marketing:** Separate campaign, separate social strategy — Miss.Princess voice throughout.

## PART 2 — COLLECTION ARCHITECTURE

Every collection — regardless of type — follows this structural framework:

### The 5 Elements of a Collection

**01 — The Collection Name**
The single phrase that holds the entire collection. Created before products are selected. The name is the world — products must fit into it, not the other way around.

**02 — The Collection Story**
A 2–3 sentence editorial statement that defines the emotional world of this collection. Written in MVQUEEN voice. Used across all marketing.
> Example: *"Soft Reign is for the woman who has stopped waiting for permission to feel elevated. Three weeks of warmth distilled into a collection built around one feeling: arrival."*

**03 — The Hero Product**
Every collection has one hero — the product that anchors the story. It is featured first, photographed most, and mentioned in every introduction. The hero earns its position through its emotional resonance, not its margin.

**04 — The Supporting Cast**
2–10 supporting products that complete the world. They each have their own story but are positioned in relation to the hero and each other.

**05 — The Campaign Visual Direction**
A single mood reference that governs every image in the collection — color grading, lighting direction, prop styling, model direction, and composition. Defined before photography begins. (Detailed in Photography_Direction.md)

## PART 4 — COLLECTION BUILD PROCESS

### Step 01 — Name First
The collection name is decided before any product selection. If the name doesn't create an atmosphere on its own — it's not the right name.

### Step 02 — Story Before Catalog
Write the 2–3 sentence collection story before building the product list. The story defines what belongs in the collection and what doesn't.

### Step 03 — Hero Product Selection
One product is designated the hero. If the collection cannot identify a clear hero — the collection needs restructuring.

### Step 04 — Supporting Product Selection
Products are selected based on how well they live inside the collection's emotional world — not purely on margin or inventory. A product that doesn't fit the world doesn't enter this collection.

### Step 05 — Visual Direction Brief
The campaign visual direction is documented before photography. Color grading reference, lighting direction, prop direction, model brief. (Use photography_direction.md as the base, then customize per collection.)

### Step 06 — Copy Before Launch
All collection copy — hero story, product descriptions, campaign headlines, email copy, social captions — is written and voice-reviewed before launch. No copy is improvised at launch.

### Step 07 — Launch Sequence
1. Customer list early access (48 hours before public)
2. Public launch
3. Week 1: hero product focus
4. Week 2: supporting product focus
5. Week 3: ritual/how-to content
6. Final week: sell-through messaging if applicable

## PART 5 — COLLECTION RETIREMENT

Collections don't die — they evolve. When a collection's active campaign lifecycle ends:

- **Core products** move into the evergreen Core Collection
- **Seasonal products** are retired until the season returns (if they performed) or retired permanently
- **Drop products** are retired permanently — limited availability is the point
- **Collection pages** are archived but not deleted (SEO value)
- **Campaign assets** are archived in the OS for reference

Nothing is deleted without a brand review. A product that underperformed in one collection context may perform differently in another.

## PART 6 — SHOPIFY COLLECTION STRUCTURE

On Shopify, collections are organized as:

```
MVQUEEN Store
├── Core Collection (always visible)
├── Active Seasonal Collection
├── Active Drop (when live)
├── Ritual Sets
├── Skincare
├── Fragrance
├── Fashion
├── Haircare
├── Beauty
├── Lifestyle
└── Miss.Princess (separate brand section)
    ├── Miss.Princess Core
    ├── Miss.Princess Active Collection
    └── Miss.Princess Beauty
```

**Navigation rule:** The customer should never be more than 2 clicks from any product. Navigation mirrors the emotional journey — not the inventory structure.
