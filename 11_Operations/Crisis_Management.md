# 🚨 MVQUEEN — Crisis Management

---

## Purpose

Define clear protocols for managing brand, operational, and reputational crises — ensuring MVQUEEN responds with speed, intelligence, and brand integrity under pressure.

A brand that handles crises well builds more trust than a brand that never faces them.

---

## Crisis Categories

| Category | Examples | Severity |
|----------|---------|---------|
| Product quality issue | Defective batch, contamination report | Critical |
| Shipping/fulfillment failure | Mass delays, lost orders, wrong items | High |
| Social media crisis | Viral negative post, brand callout | High |
| Customer service failure | Unresolved complaint going public | Medium-High |
| Website/payment outage | Store down, checkout broken | High |
| Supplier failure | Product unavailable, quality drop | Medium |
| Data or privacy issue | Breach, unauthorized data use | Critical |
| PR/reputation crisis | Negative press, influencer fallout | High |

## Crisis Response Protocol

### Immediate (First 30 Minutes)
```
□ Identify the crisis — what exactly happened?
□ Assess scope — how many customers affected?
□ Pause any scheduled content or ads that may be tone-deaf
□ Alert all relevant team members
□ Do NOT respond publicly until response is prepared
□ Document everything from the start
```

### Response Preparation (30 Minutes to 2 Hours)
```
□ Draft response statement — honest, clear, non-defensive
□ Identify what action is being taken to resolve
□ Prepare customer communication (email, DM, public comment)
□ Identify which customers need direct outreach
□ Get second eyes on response before publishing
```

### Public Response
**Tone:** Honest, accountable, warm — never defensive or dismissive
**Format:** Acknowledge → Explain briefly → Action taken → How to get help

**Template:**
*"We're aware of [issue] affecting [customers/orders/etc.]. We take this seriously and are [specific action being taken]. If you're affected, please reach out to [contact] and we will [specific resolution]. We're sorry for [specific impact] and are working to make this right."*

### Resolution Phase
```
□ Direct outreach to all affected customers
□ Resolve every individual case
□ Document resolution and outcome
□ Post-crisis review — what happened, why, how to prevent
□ Update SOPs if process failure caused the crisis
□ Follow up with most affected customers after resolution
```

---

## Crisis Communication Rules

1. Never go silent — silence reads as guilt or indifference
2. Never be defensive — acknowledge first, explain second
3. Never argue with customers publicly — move to private resolution
4. Never delete negative comments — respond to them
5. Always be specific — vague apologies feel hollow
6. Always name the action being taken — not just the apology

---

## Product Quality Crisis Protocol

If a product quality or safety issue is identified:

```
□ Pull affected product from store immediately
□ Identify which orders contained affected product
□ Contact all affected customers proactively (before they complain)
□ Offer full refund or replacement — no questions asked
□ Investigate root cause with supplier
□ Do not restock until root cause is resolved and quality confirmed
□ Communicate transparently with community if issue became public
```
