# 👑 MVQUEEN — Product Naming System

---

## Why Product Names Matter

A product name is the first copy she reads.
Before the description. Before the price. Before the image loads.
The name carries the brand's entire emotional register in two to five words.

A weak product name forces the description to do double the work.
A strong product name makes the product feel desirable before she reads another word.

MVQUEEN product names are not labels. They are invitations.

---

## Naming Philosophy

Every MVQUEEN product name follows three principles:

**1. Emotional over functional**
The name evokes a feeling before it describes a function.
Not "Hydrating Face Serum" — "Luminous Ritual Serum."

**2. Feminine and specific**
Generic beauty names are invisible. MVQUEEN names are memorable because they are specific to the brand world.
Not "Body Butter" — "Velvet Body Butter" or "Honey Glow Body Butter."

**3. Aspirational without being unattainable**
The name should make her feel like this product belongs in her life — not in someone else's.

## The Four Naming Frameworks

### FRAMEWORK 01 — The Sensory Modifier
**Structure:** [Sensory adjective] + [Product type]
**Use:** When the texture, finish, or sensation is the primary differentiator

**Approved sensory modifiers:**
Velvet · Silk · Soft · Warm · Honey · Pearl · Satin · Dewy · Luminous · Mist · Cloud · Warm

**Examples:**
- Velvet Body Butter
- Silk Hair Serum
- Honey Glow Mask
- Pearl Lip Gloss
- Warm Ritual Oil

---

### FRAMEWORK 02 — The Ritual Name
**Structure:** [Ritual moment or action] + [Product type] OR [Product type] + [Ritual descriptor]
**Use:** When the product belongs to a specific moment in her day

**Examples:**
- Morning Ritual Body Oil
- Soft Ritual Face Mist
- Evening Restore Serum
- Pre-Glow Exfoliant
- Ritual Cleansing Balm

---

### FRAMEWORK 03 — The Identity Name
**Structure:** [Identity or persona descriptor] + [Product type]
**Use:** When the product is strongly tied to a specific MVQUEEN persona or emotional state

**Examples:**
- Queen Glow Highlighter
- Soft Power Body Cream
- Elevated Eye Palette
- Feminine Ritual Kit
- Quiet Confidence Perfume Oil

---

### FRAMEWORK 04 — The Atmosphere Name
**Structure:** [Atmospheric noun or concept] + [Product type]
**Use:** For fragrance, candles, lifestyle products, and mood-based items

**Examples:**
- Warm Amber Eau de Parfum
- Golden Hour Body Oil
- Soft Evening Mist
- First Light Serum
- Candlelit Skin Balm

## Miss.Princess Naming Distinction

Miss.Princess names share the sensory quality but lean into dreaminess and playfulness.

| MVQUEEN | Miss.Princess |
|---------|--------------|
| Velvet Body Butter | Bubble Cloud Body Cream |
| Warm Ritual Oil | Dreamy Glow Oil |
| Quiet Confidence Perfume | Princess Aura Mist |
| Luminous Serum | Fairy Glow Serum |

Both sound premium. MVQUEEN sounds refined. Miss.Princess sounds dreamy.

---

## What MVQUEEN Product Names Never Include

| Avoid | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Numbers as primary identifiers (Serum #1) | Cold, clinical, forgettable |
| Overly scientific ingredient names as titles | Creates distance, not desire |
| Generic category names alone (Body Lotion) | Invisible — no brand signature |
| All caps names (GLOW SERUM) | Aggressive register |
| Names that already exist as major brand names | Confusion and legal risk |
| Names longer than 5 words | Loses impact |

---

## The Name Test

Before finalizing any product name, run it through four questions:

1. **Does it sound like MVQUEEN?** — Could it belong to a mass market brand? If yes, refine.
2. **Does it evoke a feeling?** — What does she feel when she reads it?
3. **Is it memorable?** — Would she be able to recall it 24 hours later?
4. **Does it work visually on packaging?** — Short enough for a label, beautiful enough for a box.
