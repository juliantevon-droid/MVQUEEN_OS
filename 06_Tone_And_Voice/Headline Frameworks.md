# 🗞️ MVQUEEN — Headline Frameworks

---

## What Makes a MVQUEEN Headline

A MVQUEEN headline stops her without tricking her.
It creates immediate desire, recognition, or curiosity —
and it delivers on whatever it promises.

Headlines in MVQUEEN's world are never:
- Shock-based
- Shame-based
- Vague to the point of meaning nothing
- Overly clever at the expense of clarity

They are always:
- Identity-specific
- Emotionally precise
- Confident without being aggressive
- Beautiful to read aloud

---

## FRAMEWORK 01 — The Identity Statement

She reads this and thinks: *that's me.*

**Formula:** [Who she is] + [what that means about her]

**Examples:**
- "For the woman who takes her time."
- "She doesn't explain her glow. She just has it."
- "Not high-maintenance. High-intentioned."
- "The woman who chose herself first this morning."
- "She built this ritual for herself. Nobody else."

---

## FRAMEWORK 02 — The Specific Truth

A single observation so precise it creates immediate recognition.

**Formula:** [Hyper-specific detail] + [the emotional truth underneath it]

**Examples:**
- "Two drops. That's her whole secret."
- "Her skin looks different on the weeks she uses this."
- "The mascara she reaches for when she wants her eyes to do the talking."
- "She noticed a difference by morning three."
- "The body butter she puts on before she's ready to share it."

---

## FRAMEWORK 03 — The Contrast Open

Sets up what this is NOT — then lands what it IS.

**Formula:** Not [the expected thing]. [The MVQUEEN thing.]

**Examples:**
- "Not another serum. A ritual she actually keeps."
- "Not a new her. A more present her."
- "Not a sale. An invitation."
- "Not skincare. A morning she protects."

---

## FRAMEWORK 04 — The Quiet Observation

Something she's noticed but never heard articulated.

**Formula:** [Observation she recognizes] — stated as a quiet truth.

**Examples:**
- "The right scent changes everything about a morning."
- "Skin that feels cared for behaves differently."
- "She's not buying products. She's building a ritual."
- "The way she starts her morning is the way she enters everything."

---

## FRAMEWORK 05 — The Soft Question

Poses a question she's already been asking herself.

**Formula:** [Question she recognizes as her own]

**Examples:**
- "What if her skincare felt like the best part of her morning?"
- "What does it feel like when a product actually works?"
- "What's the ritual she keeps coming back to?"

---

## FRAMEWORK 06 — The Product as Punctuation

The product name alone — at the end of an emotional setup.
No description. The name lands the statement.

**Formula:** [Emotional buildup] → [Product Name.]

**Examples:**
- "The thing that changed her morning. MVQUEEN Glow Serum."
- "She finally found it. MVQUEEN Velvet Body Butter."
- "Her skin, elevated. MVQUEEN Luminous Oil."

---

## Headline Length Guide

| Format | Ideal Length | Why |
|--------|-------------|-----|
| Email subject | 30-50 characters | Preview cuts off after ~50 |
| Ad headline | 25-40 characters | Scanned in under 2 seconds |
| Blog title | 50-65 characters | SEO + click appeal |
| Social caption opener | 1 line, max 8 words | Hook before "more" |
| Product page headline | Product name only or 5-8 words | She already clicked — now she reads |
