# 📧 MVQUEEN — Email Voice Guide

---

## How MVQUEEN Sounds in Email

Email is the most intimate channel MVQUEEN has.
It lands in her personal space — not her feed, not a search result.
Her inbox. That's a privilege.

MVQUEEN email voice honors that privilege by sounding like a message from someone who knows her — not a campaign from a company that wants her money.

The goal of every email: make her feel something before she clicks anything.

---

## Email Voice Dimensions

| Element | MVQUEEN Email | Never |
|---------|--------------|-------|
| Opening | Personal, scene-setting, emotionally grounded | "We're excited to share..." |
| Tone | Warm, intimate, woman-to-woman | Corporate, promotional, pressured |
| Length | Right-sized for the purpose — never padded | Long for the sake of appearing thorough |
| Subject line | Intriguing, identity-based, or emotionally specific | "SALE — 30% off everything!!!" |
| CTA | Soft, inviting, one per email | Multiple competing CTAs |
| Closing | Warm, signature-feel | "The MVQUEEN Team" (cold) |

## Subject Line Formulas

**Identity-based (she sees herself)**
- The woman who takes her time →
- She built her morning around this.
- Not every woman will get it. She will.
- For the one who chooses herself first.

**Curiosity-based (she needs to open)**
- Something changed in her routine this week.
- She almost didn't share this one.
- The product she keeps going back to.
- We made something for her quiet hours.

**Occasion-based (specific moment)**
- Her Friday ritual starts here.
- Sunday was made for this.
- A gift she didn't know she needed.

**Product/launch-based (direct with texture)**
- [Product Name] — finally here.
- The collection she's been waiting for.
- New. And it's everything we hoped for.
- She asked for this. We listened.

**Re-engagement (soft, personal)**
- She's been on our mind.
- Something new just arrived — we thought of her.
- It's been a moment. Here's what she missed.

---

## Preview Text Principles

Preview text should complement the subject — never repeat it.
If the subject creates intrigue, preview text adds warmth.
If the subject is direct, preview text adds texture.

**Examples:**
- Subject: "The collection she's been waiting for."
  Preview: "Built for her rituals. Available now."

- Subject: "Something changed in her routine this week."
  Preview: "Two drops. That's all it took."

- Subject: "For the one who chooses herself first."
  Preview: "A ritual designed around her, not her schedule."

## Email Opening Lines (by type)

**Welcome email**
- She made a decision today — and it suits her.
- Something is different about this place. She'll feel it.
- MVQUEEN was built for a woman like her.

**Product launch**
- This one has been in development for a while. She was worth the wait.
- She asked for something that felt like [emotion]. Here it is.
- New to the collection — and already a ritual staple.

**Promotional**
- This is not a sale email. It's an invitation.
- The collection is available — at a price that makes sense right now.
- A moment to shop what she's been saving.

**Content / editorial**
- She started her morning differently last week. This is why.
- There is a way to [outcome]. She found it.
- Some rituals take five minutes. Some change the whole day.

**Re-engagement / winback**
- It's been a little while. Things have changed here — beautifully.
- She doesn't need a reason to come back. But we have one anyway.
- Something new just arrived. We thought of her immediately.

## Body Copy Rules

**Rule 1 — One message per email**
Every email has one emotional purpose and one primary CTA.
Not three products and four links and two banners. One thing. Done beautifully.

**Rule 2 — Short paragraphs**
Two to three sentences maximum per paragraph. She's reading on her phone.
White space is not wasted — it lets the copy breathe.

**Rule 3 — Lead with feeling, end with product**
She should feel something in the first two lines before she sees a product name.

**Rule 4 — No exclamation marks in body copy**
The voice is warm — not excitable. Enthusiasm shows in word choice, not punctuation.
Exception: SMS only, and even then, sparingly.

**Rule 5 — She, not you**
MVQUEEN email often refers to the customer in third person ("she") in narrative passages
before shifting to second person ("you") for direct address.
This creates a mirror effect — she sees a woman she recognizes.

## What Never Appears in MVQUEEN Email

- "We're excited to announce" — start with the thing
- Excessive emoji use — one maximum, and only if it fits naturally
- Countdown timers on regular sends — reserved for specific drops only
- Guilt-based re-engagement language ("You haven't shopped in a while...")
- Generic social proof ("Join thousands of happy customers") — be specific or omit
- More than one CTA button per email — choose one
- ALL CAPS anywhere in body copy
- Excessive exclamation marks — never in body copy
