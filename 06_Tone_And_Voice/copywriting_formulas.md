# ✍️ MVQUEEN — Copywriting Formulas

## How to Use This File

These are proven structural formulas adapted specifically for MVQUEEN's voice.
Every formula has a template, the MVQUEEN version, and a filled example.

A formula is a skeleton. Your voice and brand intelligence are the flesh.
Fill it in. Make it feel alive. Then it becomes MVQUEEN.

---

## FORMULA 01 — Feel First, Product Second

**Structure:**
> [The feeling she wants] → [What creates that feeling] → [The product]

**Template:**
```
[Emotional state or sensory experience she's reaching for.]
[What that moment looks like or feels like.]
[Product name] was made for exactly this.
```

**Example:**
```
The kind of morning that belongs entirely to you.
Quiet. Unhurried. Warm light and no obligations.
MVQUEEN Soft Ritual Body Oil was made for exactly this.
```

**Use for:** Product descriptions, email openers, social captions, ad copy

## FORMULA 02 — The Identity Mirror

**Structure:**
> [Who she is] → [What she wants] → [How this fits her]

**Template:**
```
She doesn't chase trends. She sets the atmosphere.
[Specific desire or standard she holds.]
[Product] — made for her kind of [ritual/morning/evening/presence].
```

**Example:**
```
She doesn't chase trends. She sets the atmosphere.
Her beauty ritual is intentional, not performative.
MVQUEEN Luminous Lip Set — made for her kind of presence.
```

**Use for:** Product pages, ads, collection descriptions, social

---

## FORMULA 03 — The Sensory Walk

**Structure:**
> [Touch] → [Sight] → [Feeling] → [Result]

**Template:**
```
[Texture or physical sensation on application.]
[What she sees immediately after.]
[How she feels wearing it / using it.]
[The lasting impression it creates.]
```

**Example:**
```
Weightless on the skin — like a second layer of warmth.
A finish that catches light the way candlelight does.
She feels settled. Present. Soft in the best way.
The kind of glow that doesn't ask for attention — it simply has it.
```

**Use for:** Product descriptions, unboxing content, email campaigns

## FORMULA 04 — The Quiet Flex

**Structure:**
> [What others notice] → [What she knows] → [The product behind it]

**Template:**
```
They'll notice something different about her.
She'll know exactly what it is.
[Product name.] [One-line description.]
```

**Example:**
```
They'll notice something different about her skin today.
She'll know exactly what changed.
MVQUEEN Glow Serum. Two drops. Every morning. That's the whole secret.
```

**Use for:** Ads, social captions, email subject lines, product teasers

## FORMULA 05 — The Ritual Frame

**Structure:**
> [Time of day or moment] → [The action] → [The feeling it creates] → [The invitation]

**Template:**
```
[Specific time or setting — morning, evening, before she leaves, just after her shower.]
[She reaches for / she layers / she applies.]
[What that moment becomes because of it.]
[Invitation — soft, identity-based.]
```

**Example:**
```
Just after her shower. Still warm. Still quiet.
She reaches for the Velvet Body Butter and takes her time.
The whole morning shifts — slower, softer, more hers.
This is what a ritual feels like.
```

**Use for:** Product descriptions, email campaigns, social, video scripts

## FORMULA 06 — The Contrast Open

**Structure:**
> [What it's not] → [What it is] → [Product]

**Template:**
```
Not another [generic category product].
[What makes this fundamentally different — emotionally or experientially.]
[Product name] — [short evocative descriptor].
```

**Example:**
```
Not another lip gloss.
A mood. A finish. A reason she keeps reaching for it.
MVQUEEN Glass Lip — effortless shine, all day.
```

**Use for:** Ads, product page intros, social captions, launch copy

## FORMULA 07 — The Permission Statement

**Structure:**
> [Give her permission to want this] → [Normalize the desire] → [The product]

**Template:**
```
She can want beautiful things for no reason other than they're beautiful.
[Expand the permission — normalize enjoying luxury without guilt.]
[Product name] is that kind of beautiful.
```

**Example:**
```
She can want soft, beautiful skin just because it makes her feel good.
No occasion required. No explanation needed.
MVQUEEN Honey Glow Mask is that kind of beautiful.
```

**Use for:** Email campaigns, collection descriptions, brand content

## FORMULA 08 — The Short Punch (for ads and hooks)

**Structure:**
> [Bold observation or truth] → [Product] → [CTA]

**Template:**
```
[One line — something she already believes but hasn't heard said this way.]
[Product name] — [3-5 word descriptor.]
[Soft CTA.]
```

**Example:**
```
Your morning sets the tone for everything that follows.
MVQUEEN Morning Ritual Set — start it beautifully.
Shop the collection →
```

**Use for:** Paid ads, TikTok hooks, email subject lines, story text

---

## FORMULA 09 — The Before / After (Without Pressure)

**Structure:**
> [Where she is before] → [Where she is after] → [What made the difference]

**MVQUEEN rule:** The "before" is never shame-based. It's neutral or relatable.
The "after" is emotional elevation, not a physical transformation standard.

**Template:**
```
[Neutral before — she's fine, just not quite feeling her best.]
[After — she feels more like herself. Settled. Luminous. Present.]
[Product name] is the difference.
```

**Example:**
```
Some mornings she moves through the world a little disconnected.
Then she takes five minutes. Just for her.
MVQUEEN Rose Ritual Mist — the five minutes that bring her back.
```

**Use for:** Email sequences, ads, product storytelling, social

---

## FORMULA 10 — The One-Line Product Truth

**Structure:**
> [What it does] in [how it feels to use it] language.

No fluff. No setup. Just the most honest, beautiful version of what this product is.

**Examples:**
```
A serum that makes sleep feel like a beauty treatment.
The lip color she reaches for even on no-makeup days.
Dry shampoo that smells like it costs more than it does.
The mascara that makes her eyes do the talking.
Body butter so rich she almost doesn't want to put clothes on.
```

**Use for:** Product tiles, ad headlines, email preview text, Pinterest descriptions
