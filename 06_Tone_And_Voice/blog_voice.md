# 🎙️ MVQUEEN — Blog Voice

---

## Purpose

Define exactly how MVQUEEN writes blog content — with real examples, structural standards, and a voice checklist that ensures every article feels like editorial, not content marketing.

---

## The Blog Voice Standard

MVQUEEN blog content is:
- Written for a woman — not for a search algorithm
- Expert without being clinical
- Warm without being casual
- Specific without being overwhelming
- Honest about what we know and don't know

**The test:** Would a woman read this because she genuinely wants to — or only because she's searching for information?

The goal is both. But humanity comes first.

---

## Opening Standards

Blog articles never open with:
- A dictionary definition
- A statistic (unless immediately humanized)
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "Whether you're a [type] or a [type]..."
- The brand name or a product pitch

Blog articles always open with:
- A moment she recognizes
- A question she's already asking herself
- A tension or truth that immediately connects

**Wrong:**
*Skincare has been an important part of beauty culture for centuries, with women throughout history seeking ways to improve their complexion...*

**Right:**
*There's a version of your morning routine that doesn't feel like a checklist. Most women haven't found it yet — but they know it's possible.*

---

## Body Copy Standards

- Short paragraphs (3-4 lines maximum on mobile)
- Headers that create curiosity — not just label content
- Specific examples over vague generalities
- Sensory language where relevant
- One idea per paragraph — never cramming multiple points

**Wrong header:** *Benefits of Hyaluronic Acid*
**Right header:** *What Hyaluronic Acid Actually Does (Without the Science Degree)*

---

## Closing Standards

Articles never end with:
- "In conclusion..."
- A summary of what was just covered
- An abrupt stop

Articles always end with:
- An invitation — to try something, think about something, or explore something
- A brand connection that feels earned — not tacked on
- A soft CTA if relevant — never a hard sell

---

## Voice Checklist for Blog

```
□ Opening places her in a moment she recognizes?
□ Headers create curiosity — not just categories?
□ Every paragraph has one idea?
□ Specific examples used throughout — not vague generalities?
□ Closing is an invitation — not a summary?
□ Sounds like MVQUEEN — not a generic beauty blog?
□ Primary keyword used naturally — not forced?
□ Would she share this with a friend?
```
