## PRIMARY PALETTE — DETAILED

### 01 — Warm Ivory `#FAF6F0`
**Role:** The canvas. The breathing room. The emotional baseline of every MVQUEEN composition.

**What it communicates:** Openness, refined simplicity, feminine calm. Not clinical white — this ivory carries warmth. It feels like morning light through linen curtains, not a hospital wall.

**Use for:**
- All Shopify page backgrounds
- Email backgrounds
- Packaging base color (boxes, mailers)
- Whitespace in editorial layouts
- Image overlay wash (at 20–30% opacity)
- Product photography backdrop

**Never use for:**
- Body text — too low contrast
- Call-to-action buttons
- Active navigation states

---

### 02 — Soft Blush `#F2D4CC`
**Role:** Warmth layer. The color that makes the brand feel feminine and intimate rather than cold and neutral.

**What it communicates:** Tenderness, beauty, emotional closeness. The blush tones in MVQUEEN are mature — not cotton candy, not millennial pink. Think pressed powder, not bubble gum.

**Use for:**
- Homepage section backgrounds (alternating with Warm Ivory)
- Email hero sections
- Social media accent blocks
- Campaign backgrounds
- Packaging interior tissue paper
- Product card hover states (Shopify)
- Beauty category pages

**Never use for:**
- Dark-mode contexts
- Text color
- Technical documentation

---

### 03 — Dusty Rose `#C9968A`
**Role:** Depth and sophistication. Where Soft Blush is warmth, Dusty Rose is maturity.

**What it communicates:** Grounded femininity, emotional richness, quiet confidence. This is the color of the brand when she has something important to say.

**Use for:**
- CTAs when placed on light backgrounds
- Section dividers and accent lines
- Campaign headline color (in editorial contexts)
- Icon fills and small UI elements
- Social highlight covers
- Packaging ribbon or closure detail

**Minimum contrast use:** Always pair with Warm Ivory or white backgrounds. Do not place on Soft Blush — insufficient contrast.

---

### 04 — Warm Taupe `#C4B5A5`
**Role:** The stabilizer. Sits between the warm backgrounds and the deep accents without disrupting either.

**What it communicates:** Restraint, intentionality, timeless sophistication. Taupe is the color of cashmere, of well-chosen things. It doesn't shout — it holds the room together.

**Use for:**
- UI borders and dividers
- Input field outlines (Shopify forms)
- Secondary button borders
- Photography prop tones
- Packaging secondary detail
- Inactive navigation
- Footer background tint

---

### 05 — Warm Beige `#E8DDD0`
**Role:** Secondary background. Softer than Taupe, warmer than Ivory. Used to create visual layering without stark contrast.

**Use for:**
- Alternating section backgrounds
- Card backgrounds
- Product image backgrounds (when not using Ivory)
- Email section separators
- Sidebar backgrounds

---

### 06 — Espresso `#3B2314`
**Role:** Typography anchor. The deepest tone in the palette. Grounds every composition.

**What it communicates:** Richness, confidence, maturity, depth. Espresso is warm — not cold black, not flat grey. It feels like dark chocolate and worn leather. It reads as luxury without aggression.

**Use for:**
- All primary body text
- Headlines on light backgrounds
- Logo primary color
- Button text on light CTAs
- Icon outlines
- Packaging typography
- Price display text

**Never use:**
- As a background color (except in small accent blocks for contrast moments)
- For large areas of UI — it's a typography and detail color, not a fill color

**Accessibility:** At `#3B2314` on `#FAF6F0` background — contrast ratio 12.8:1. Passes WCAG AAA. ✅

---

### 07 — Champagne Gold `#D4AF78`
**Role:** The luxury signal. Used sparingly — every use must earn its placement.

**What it communicates:** Elevation, refinement, quiet glamour. This is not yellow gold — it's warm, muted, sophisticated. The difference between a Rolex face and a costume jewelry chain.

**Use for:**
- Logo accent element (foil or color variant)
- Packaging foil detail, embossing, closure hardware
- Campaign "hero" accent — one element per visual
- Crown icon in MVQUEEN branding
- Premium product callouts
- Loyalty tier indicators

**Never use for:**
- Body text
- Background fills
- More than one element per composition
- Miss.Princess primary branding (she uses silver/pearl tones instead)

---

### 08 — Soft Charcoal `#4A4240`
**Role:** Secondary text and subtle contrast. Warmer than grey, lighter than Espresso.

**Use for:**
- Subheadings and secondary text
- Captions and metadata
- Supporting UI elements
- Descriptive text in product listings
- Footer text

## COLOR USAGE RULES

### Rule 01 — The 40/20/10/10/10/5/3/2 Ratio
Follow the usage percentages in the palette table. Ivory dominates. Blush accents. Everything else supports. Champagne Gold appears last and earns the most attention.

### Rule 02 — Warm Always Wins
When choosing between a warm neutral and a cool neutral — always choose warm. Cool grey, cool white, cool beige do not belong in MVQUEEN compositions. If it has a blue or green undertone, it's wrong.

### Rule 03 — Metallics Never Fill
Champagne Gold and Pearl are accent colors only. They exist as lines, details, foils, and single-element highlights. A gold button is fine. A gold section background is not.

### Rule 04 — No Pure Black. Ever.
Pure black (`#000000`) does not appear in MVQUEEN branding. It's too cold, too harsh, too disconnected from the warmth of the palette. Espresso `#3B2314` replaces it in every context.

### Rule 05 — No Pure White. Ever.
Pure white (`#FFFFFF`) is replaced by Warm Ivory `#FAF6F0` in all brand contexts. White feels clinical. Ivory feels considered.

### Rule 06 — High Contrast for Accessibility
All text/background combinations must pass WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 ratio for body text, 3:1 for large text). Espresso on Ivory passes AAA. Dusty Rose on Ivory passes AA. Test every combination before implementing.

### Rule 07 — Three Colors Maximum Per Composition
Any single visual — social post, email section, product card — uses a maximum of three palette colors. Background + accent + typography. Adding a fourth creates visual noise.

## PHOTOGRAPHY COLOR GRADING BRIEF

When editing MVQUEEN photography:

**Target feel:** Warm, cinematic, softly luminous. Like golden hour held a moment longer than it should have.

**Grading direction:**
- Lift shadows slightly — no crushed blacks
- Warm highlights toward champagne/ivory tones
- Desaturate cool tones (blues, greens) by 15–20%
- Add warmth to skin tones — lean amber, not orange
- Keep contrast soft — this is not editorial drama, it's intimate beauty
- Grain: fine film grain at 5–10% adds depth without looking filtered

**Avoid:**
- Cool/moody grading (grey shadows, blue tones)
- Heavy contrast or dark vignettes
- Over-saturated skin or product colors
- Faded/washed out "airy" presets — we want warmth, not absence of color

**LUT direction:** Think Kodak Portra 400 warmth — natural, rich, slightly soft.
