# 🪝 MVQUEEN — Hook Systems

---

## What a Hook Does

A hook is the first thing she encounters — and it decides everything.

If the hook lands: she reads the caption, watches the video, opens the email.
If it doesn't: she moves on in under two seconds.

MVQUEEN hooks are not clickbait.
They earn attention honestly — through emotional precision, unexpected truth, or immediate recognition.

---

## The Four Hook Mechanisms

**1. Recognition** — She thinks: *that's exactly how I feel.*
**2. Curiosity** — She thinks: *I need to know what comes next.*
**3. Desire** — She thinks: *I want whatever that is.*
**4. Disruption** — She thinks: *wait, that's different.*

Every strong MVQUEEN hook triggers at least one of these.
The best ones trigger two.

---

## HOOK TYPE 01 — The Recognition Hook

She reads the first line and feels seen.

**Formula:** [Something she's felt but never heard said exactly this way]

**Examples:**
- "She changed one thing in her routine. Everything followed."
- "There's a version of her morning that doesn't feel like maintenance."
- "She's not tired. She's just ready to be taken care of for once."
- "Her skin looks best on the weeks she actually slows down."
- "She stopped chasing glow and started building it."

---

## HOOK TYPE 02 — The Curiosity Hook

She has to find out what comes next.

**Formula:** [Incomplete statement or intriguing setup that demands a follow]

**Examples:**
- "She almost didn't share this one."
- "Three things changed in her routine this month. One of them surprised her."
- "The product she was skeptical about — and then couldn't stop using."
- "Her skin looked different and she couldn't explain why. Then she could."
- "What she puts on before anything else. Every morning."

---

## HOOK TYPE 03 — The Desire Hook

She sees or reads something and immediately wants it.

**Formula:** [Outcome so specific and appealing she imagines herself in it]

**Examples:**
- "Skin so soft she'll keep touching it."
- "The scent she'll name her whole morning after."
- "The kind of glow that doesn't need a filter."
- "Hair that moves the way she wants to — finally."
- "A lip color she reaches for even on no-makeup days."

---

## HOOK TYPE 04 — The Disruption Hook

She's heard the category claim a hundred times — this version breaks the pattern.

**Formula:** [Subvert the expected statement] or [Say the thing nobody says]

**Examples:**
- "Not a 10-step routine. A two-drop ritual."
- "Skincare that doesn't ask her to become someone else."
- "She didn't add more. She refined what she had."
- "No before. Just an after that makes sense."
- "The brand that didn't tell her she had a problem."

---

## TikTok / Reel Hook Formats

Video hooks must work visually AND as text overlay.
The first 3 seconds determine everything.

**Text + Visual hooks:**
- [Text]: "The reason she looks like THAT every morning" + [Visual: her ritual, slow-mo]
- [Text]: "POV: you finally found your skincare routine" + [Visual: product reveal]
- [Text]: "She switched one thing. Here's what happened." + [Visual: before/during/after ritual]
- [Text]: "What's in her actual morning routine (no sponsored products)" + [Visual: shelfie walk-through]
- [Text]: "The glow she keeps getting asked about" + [Visual: skin close-up in natural light]

**Verbal hooks (first spoken words):**
- "Okay I need to talk about this serum because —"
- "This is the ritual I've been building for six months and I finally got it right."
- "She asked me what I use on my skin. Every single day this week. So here it is."
- "Stop skipping body care. This is why it matters."

## Email Subject Line Hooks

**Recognition:**
- "She knows this feeling."
- "The one she keeps coming back to."
- "For the morning she claims as hers."

**Curiosity:**
- "She almost didn't share this one."
- "Something changed in her routine this month."
- "The product her friends keep asking about."

**Desire:**
- "Her ritual, elevated."
- "What her skin does when she uses this."
- "The collection she's been waiting for."

**Disruption:**
- "Not a sale. Something better."
- "She stopped adding products. Then she found this one."
- "Not another skincare routine. A ritual she actually keeps."

---

## Hook Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why | Instead |
|---------|-----|---------|
| "You won't believe this!" | Clickbait — creates distrust | Be specific about what's surprising |
| "This changed my life!" | Overblown — creates skepticism | Name the specific change |
| "POV: you have good skin" | Vague aspiration | Name the ritual or the product |
| Starting with the brand name | She doesn't care yet | Start with her |
| Starting with the product name | Misses the hook entirely | Start with what the product creates |
