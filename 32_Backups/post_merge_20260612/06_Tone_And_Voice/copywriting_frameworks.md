# 🧩 MVQUEEN — Copywriting Frameworks

---

## Frameworks vs. Formulas

Formulas (in `copywriting_formulas.md`) are sentence-level structures.
Frameworks are full piece-level architectures — how an entire email, ad campaign, or content piece is built from beginning to end.

Use formulas to write individual lines.
Use frameworks to build complete pieces.

---

## FRAMEWORK 01 — The Emotional Arc

**Best for:** Email campaigns, long-form captions, product storytelling, blog posts

**Structure:**
```
TENSION (she feels something missing or incomplete)
    ↓
RECOGNITION (MVQUEEN names what she's reaching for)
    ↓
SOLUTION (the product or ritual that closes the gap)
    ↓
IDENTITY (who she becomes, or returns to, through this)
    ↓
INVITATION (one gentle action she can take)
```

**Example applied to a face oil email:**
- TENSION: "Some mornings she can't quite put her finger on what's missing."
- RECOGNITION: "It's not productivity she wants more of. It's presence."
- SOLUTION: "MVQUEEN Luminous Face Oil — two drops that tell her skin she's being taken care of."
- IDENTITY: "The woman who treats herself like she matters. Because she does."
- INVITATION: "Make it part of her morning →"

## FRAMEWORK 02 — The Brand World Frame

**Best for:** Collection launches, seasonal campaigns, editorial content, homepage copy

**Structure:**
```
WORLD BUILDING (paint the atmosphere — season, feeling, light, texture)
    ↓
INHABITANTS (describe the woman who lives in this world)
    ↓
THE COLLECTION (introduce the products as belonging to this world)
    ↓
THE INVITATION (enter this world)
```

**Example applied to a winter collection:**
- WORLD: "Shorter days. Candlelight before dinner. The kind of cold that makes warmth feel intentional."
- INHABITANTS: "She moves slower this season — and she means to."
- COLLECTION: "The MVQUEEN Winter Ritual Collection was built for this. For her."
- INVITATION: "Enter the collection →"

## FRAMEWORK 03 — The Single Truth

**Best for:** Paid ads, short social content, SMS, email subject lines, product tile copy

**Structure:**
```
ONE TRUTH (the most compelling, specific, honest thing about this product or moment)
    ↓
THE EVIDENCE (one line that makes it real — sensory, specific)
    ↓
THE PRODUCT (named simply)
    ↓
THE ACTION (one word or one line)
```

**Example:**
- TRUTH: "Her skin looks different when she sleeps in this."
- EVIDENCE: "A retinol-alternative formula that works while she rests."
- PRODUCT: "MVQUEEN Overnight Renewal Serum."
- ACTION: "Try it →"

## FRAMEWORK 04 — The Mirror Method

**Best for:** Community content, retention emails, brand storytelling, Instagram

**Structure:**
```
OBSERVATION ABOUT HER (something she's felt but never heard said this way)
    ↓
VALIDATION (MVQUEEN sees this and honors it — without hollow affirmation)
    ↓
PARALLEL (what MVQUEEN was built from — the same energy)
    ↓
CONNECTION (the thread between her and the brand)
```

**Example:**
- OBSERVATION: "She's not trying to be someone else. She's trying to be more fully herself."
- VALIDATION: "That distinction matters. A lot of brands miss it."
- PARALLEL: "MVQUEEN was built by a woman who understood that difference."
- CONNECTION: "This brand is for her. Not who she's supposed to be. Who she already is."

## FRAMEWORK 05 — The Ritual Reveal

**Best for:** Tutorial content, product-in-use content, TikTok, Reels, how-to blog posts

**Structure:**
```
THE MOMENT (specific time and setting — grounds it in her real life)
    ↓
THE REACH (she reaches for the product — make this feel intentional)
    ↓
THE APPLICATION (sensory, specific — what it feels like to use)
    ↓
THE SHIFT (what changes — emotionally, physically, atmospherically)
    ↓
THE CLOSE (her, after — one line)
```

**Example — Body Scrub:**
- MOMENT: "Saturday morning. Before she has to be anywhere."
- REACH: "She reaches for the MVQUEEN Sugar Polish. It's become the ritual she protects."
- APPLICATION: "The texture is rich — exfoliating without harshness. The scent fills the shower."
- SHIFT: "She steps out and her skin is different. Softer. More present somehow."
- CLOSE: "She doesn't rush to get dressed."

## FRAMEWORK 06 — The Contrast Campaign

**Best for:** Repositioning campaigns, competitive differentiation, brand values content

**Structure:**
```
WHAT MOST BRANDS DO (name the category norm — don't name competitors)
    ↓
WHY THAT FALLS SHORT (emotionally honest, not aggressive)
    ↓
WHAT MVQUEEN DOES DIFFERENTLY
    ↓
THE PROOF (a product, an experience, or a philosophy that demonstrates it)
```

**Example:**
- NORM: "Most beauty brands are built around transformation. Become something better."
- FALLS SHORT: "That language assumes she isn't enough yet."
- DIFFERENT: "MVQUEEN is built around recognition. She already is the woman. The ritual just helps her feel it."
- PROOF: "Every product in this collection was formulated to elevate what's already there — not replace it."

## FRAMEWORK 07 — Launch Sequence Architecture

**Best for:** New product launches across all channels

**Phase 1 — Tease (7-10 days before)**
Build desire without revealing. Use atmosphere, feeling, sensory language.
Channels: Social, email teaser, SMS to VIPs.

**Phase 2 — Reveal (3 days before)**
Name the product. Show it. Tell her what it does — in emotional language.
Channels: Social reveal post, email with details, SMS to full list.

**Phase 3 — Launch (day of)**
Available. The full story. Every channel aligned.
Channels: Email (full campaign), social (product focus), SMS, ads live.

**Phase 4 — Sustain (days 2-7)**
Keep the energy through UGC, ritual content, reviews, and re-engagement.
Channels: Social (ritual content), retargeting ads, email to non-openers.

**Phase 5 — Close (if limited)**
Soft urgency — "still available" or "nearly gone" — never panic language.
Channels: Email, SMS, social.

## Choosing a Framework

| Situation | Framework |
|-----------|-----------|
| Single product email | 01 — Emotional Arc |
| Collection or season launch | 02 — Brand World Frame |
| Paid ad | 03 — Single Truth |
| Community / brand content | 04 — Mirror Method |
| Tutorial or ritual content | 05 — Ritual Reveal |
| Differentiation campaign | 06 — Contrast Campaign |
| New product launch | 07 — Launch Sequence |
