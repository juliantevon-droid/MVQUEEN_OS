# 📣 MVQUEEN — Ad Copy Voice Guide

---

## Ads Are a First Impression

Most women who see a MVQUEEN ad have never heard of the brand.
In 2-3 seconds, the ad must:
- Stop the scroll
- Create desire or recognition
- Make her want to know more

MVQUEEN ads don't shout. They magnetize.
The goal is never to interrupt — it is to belong in her feed so naturally
that she stops because she wants to, not because she had to.

---

## Ad Voice Principles

**1. Hook is everything**
The first line, first frame, or first visual must earn the next second.
If it doesn't stop her, nothing else matters.

**2. Emotion before information**
She feels it before she reads it. The feeling is the message.
Features and details come after — if at all.

**3. Identity over urgency**
She buys because of who she is, not because of how little time she has.
Speak to her identity. Never to her fear.

**4. Specificity creates trust**
Vague claims are invisible. Specific details are magnetic.
Not "amazing results" — "skin that glows before she puts makeup on."

**5. One message per ad**
One feeling. One product. One action.
The ad that tries to say everything says nothing.

## Ad Formats and Voice by Type

### Static Image Ad (Facebook / Instagram)

**Headline formula:**
```
[Identity statement OR sensory detail OR bold truth]
```

**Examples:**
- She built her glow from the inside out.
- Two drops. That's the whole routine.
- The kind of skin that makes her feel like herself again.
- Soft. Intentional. Unmistakably hers.
- She doesn't explain her glow. She just has it.

**Body copy formula:**
```
[Expand the feeling — 1-2 sentences]
[Product name + what it does in emotional language]
[Soft CTA]
```

**Example:**
```
She doesn't follow trends. She sets her own ritual.
MVQUEEN Luminous Face Oil — 3 drops, every morning, no exceptions.
Make it yours →
```

---

### Video / Reel Ad (15-30 seconds)

**Hook (0-3 seconds):**
Visual or audio that creates immediate recognition or desire.
Must work on mute AND with sound.

**Hook examples:**
- [Visual: her morning ritual, slow motion, warm light] + text: "Her secret isn't a product."
- [Product reveal] + voiceover: "She's been asked about her skin every day this week."
- [Close-up texture] + text: "Two drops changed her whole morning."

**Middle (3-20 seconds):**
Show the product in context — her world, her ritual, her result.
Not a clinical demo. A feeling.

**Close (last 5 seconds):**
Brand name + product name + CTA.
Simple. Clean. Confident.

---

### Carousel Ad

Each slide carries a single idea. Together they tell one story.

**Carousel flow example:**
- Slide 1: The hook — emotion or bold statement
- Slide 2: The ritual — how she uses it
- Slide 3: The result — what she feels
- Slide 4: The product — clean reveal
- Slide 5: The CTA — "Shop the collection"

---

### Retargeting Ad

She's seen MVQUEEN before. This ad reminds her why she stopped.

**Tone:** Softer. More personal. Assumes familiarity.

**Examples:**
```
She was looking at this for a reason.
[Product name] — still here when she's ready. →
```

```
Some decisions take a moment.
She's had her moment. The collection is waiting. 👑
```

## Headline Swipe File (Tested Formats)

**Identity-based:**
- She built her ritual around this.
- For the woman who takes her time.
- The glow she stopped trying to explain.
- Made for her kind of morning.
- Not every woman gets it. She does.

**Sensory-based:**
- Skin so soft she'll keep touching it.
- A scent she'll name her mood after.
- The finish that catches light like nothing else.
- Weightless. Luminous. Unmistakably her.

**Curiosity-based:**
- She changed one thing in her routine. Everything followed.
- The product her friends keep asking about.
- What her skin looks like before makeup.
- She almost didn't share this one.

**Product truth:**
- Two drops. Every morning. That's the ritual.
- [Product name] — the one she reaches for first.
- Her skin is doing the most. The secret: [product name].

## Ad Copy Never Includes

- Exclamation marks in headlines (they signal cheap urgency)
- "Limited time" language on standard ads
- Before/after with shame-based before
- Claims that can't be substantiated
- Competitor comparisons
- Generic beauty category language ("glowing skin," "beautiful you")
- All caps in body copy
- More than one CTA
