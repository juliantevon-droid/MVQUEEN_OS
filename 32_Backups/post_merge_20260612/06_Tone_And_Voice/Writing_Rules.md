# 👑 MVQUEEN — Writing Rules
### The Craft Manual

---

## What This File Governs

Voice is *what* MVQUEEN sounds like.
Writing Rules are *how* that voice gets built at the sentence level.

This file is for anyone who writes for MVQUEEN — humans and AI systems alike. It covers sentence mechanics, structure, rhythm, formatting, and the specific craft decisions that separate MVQUEEN copy from everything else in the market.

Follow these rules and the voice will hold.
Ignore them and it won't matter how good your intentions were.

---

## PART 1 — SENTENCE MECHANICS

### Rule 01 — Active Voice. Always.
Passive voice removes the brand from the experience. MVQUEEN does not happen *to* women. It happens *with* them and *for* them.

> ❌ "This serum was formulated to restore radiance."
> ✅ "This serum restores radiance — and reminds skin what it's capable of."

Passive voice is only permitted in legal disclaimers and ingredient disclosures.

---

### Rule 02 — Vary Sentence Length Deliberately
Short sentences create impact. Medium sentences carry information. Long sentences create rhythm and immersion. MVQUEEN copy uses all three — never one at the expense of the others.

The pattern that works most reliably:
> Long sentence that creates atmosphere. Short sentence that lands the point. Medium sentence that extends it naturally.

Example:
> "Getting dressed for yourself — not for anyone watching — is one of the most quietly powerful things a woman can do. MVQUEEN was made for that moment. The one that exists before the world gets to see it."

---

### Rule 03 — The Em Dash Is Your Best Structural Tool
The em dash (—) creates a natural pause, adds a layer of meaning, and gives copy a rhythmic sophistication that commas and colons don't.

> "The kind of confidence that arrives quietly — and stays."
> "Not a fragrance. An atmosphere — one you carry with you."

Use it. Don't overuse it. Maximum two em dashes per paragraph.

---

### Rule 04 — The Line Break Is Not Decoration
In MVQUEEN copy — especially in social captions, product descriptions, and campaign copy — line breaks are punctuation. They create visual breathing room and control how the reader moves through the copy.

> "She doesn't wait for permission.
> She doesn't perform confidence.
> She simply already knows."

A line break tells the reader: *pause here. Let this land.*

Do not use line breaks in email body copy or website paragraphs — only in display copy and social.

---

### Rule 05 — Periods Inside Thought Fragments Are Intentional
MVQUEEN copy sometimes uses fragment sentences — incomplete grammatically, complete emotionally. This is intentional style, not an error.

> "For the woman who already knows. For the one who's still learning. For both."

Fragments work when they accumulate. They fail when they replace complete thought. Use them for emotional punctuation, not as a substitute for writing actual sentences.

---

### Rule 06 — Eliminate Filler Qualifiers
These words make copy softer in the wrong way — they dilute confidence without adding warmth:

Remove: *very, really, quite, just, a bit, somewhat, kind of, sort of, a little, actually, truly, truly truly*

> ❌ "This is actually a really beautiful fragrance."
> ✅ "This fragrance does what it says. Beautifully."

---

### Rule 07 — Numbers in Copy
- Spell out numbers one through nine: *three, seven, nine*
- Use numerals for 10 and above: *12, 45, 100*
- Always use numerals for percentages, measurements, and prices: *30ml, $68, 15%*
- Times of day: *7 AM, noon, 11 PM* — not "seven in the morning"

## PART 2 — STRUCTURE

### Rule 08 — Every Product Description Has Three Jobs
In this order:
1. **Create desire** — before the product is named, she should want the feeling it delivers
2. **Describe the product** — what it is, what it contains, what it does
3. **Confirm the identity** — remind her why it belongs in her world specifically

> "Getting ready shouldn't feel like preparation. It should feel like ritual. [PRODUCT NAME] is a [what it is] that [what it does] — [sensory detail]. [Key ingredient/feature]. Made for the woman who treats self-care as a standard, not a reward."

---

### Rule 09 — Email Copy Structure
Every MVQUEEN email follows this emotional arc regardless of its purpose:

1. **Subject line** — creates intrigue or warmth, never urgency or discounting
2. **Opening line** — emotional hook; she should feel something before she reads anything
3. **Body** — product/offer/story told through experience, not announcement
4. **CTA** — one. Single. Not three buttons competing for attention.
5. **Sign-off** — warm, personal, on-brand. Never "The MVQUEEN Team." Sign as *MVQUEEN* or from a named brand voice.

---

### Rule 10 — Social Caption Structure
Instagram and long-form social captions follow this structure:

1. **Hook (line 1)** — stops the scroll; emotional, direct, or atmospheric
2. **Body (2–5 lines)** — develops the idea, story, or product context
3. **Landing line** — the line that stays with her after she scrolls away
4. **CTA (optional)** — gentle, not aggressive; one action only
5. **Hashtags** — below a line break, never embedded in the caption body

---

### Rule 11 — Campaign Copy Structure
For launches, collections, and seasonal campaigns:

1. **Campaign headline** — the single most important line; tested before anything else
2. **Sub-headline** — extends the headline's emotional direction
3. **Body copy** — 2–4 sentences maximum; atmospheric, not informational
4. **Product CTA** — clean, single-focus
5. **Visual relationship** — the copy must *add* to the visual, never explain it

If the image already says something, the copy says something *else* — something deeper.

## PART 3 — FORMATTING RULES

### Rule 12 — Capitalization
- Brand name: always **MVQUEEN** — all caps, no space
- Sister brand: always **Miss.Princess** — capital M, capital P, period between
- Product names: Title Case
- Collection names: Title Case
- Headlines: Title Case for formal documents; Sentence case for conversational copy
- Do not capitalize common nouns for emphasis (*Beauty, Confidence, Woman*) — this pattern reads as generic brand speak

---

### Rule 13 — Punctuation Hierarchy in Copy
- **Period** — full stop, maximum confidence
- **Em dash (—)** — layered meaning, rhythmic pause
- **Ellipsis (...)** — use *only* to create intentional suspension; not as lazy punctuation
- **Exclamation marks** — avoid in MVQUEEN copy. Miss.Princess may use one per caption maximum.
- **Question marks** — use sparingly in body copy; effective in subject lines and hooks
- **Ampersand (&)** — permitted in product names and collection names only; not in body copy
- **Quotation marks** — for actual quotes only; not for emphasis or irony

---

### Rule 14 — Bold and Italic Use
- **Bold** — for critical product information (key ingredient, size, key claim) and document navigation only; never for emotional emphasis in customer copy
- *Italic* — for product names referenced within copy, foreign words, and titles; not for emotional emphasis
- Do not bold or italicize words in social captions or email body copy for stylistic effect — the writing should create emphasis, not the formatting

---

### Rule 15 — Header Hierarchy in Documents
For internal OS documents:
- `# H1` — document title only (one per document)
- `## H2` — major sections
- `### H3` — subsections
- `#### H4` — only if absolutely necessary; if you're at H4, consider restructuring

For customer-facing copy (product pages, website):
- No visible H tags in the final experience — use only within CMS structure

## PART 4 — MVQUEEN-SPECIFIC STYLE DECISIONS

### Rule 16 — How to Introduce the Brand Name
In copy, MVQUEEN is referred to as:
- **MVQUEEN** (primary)
- **the brand** (in longer editorial pieces, after first use)
- Never as "we" in third-person contexts (choose either first or third person and commit)
- Never as "MVQUEEN brand" — redundant

---

### Rule 17 — How to Reference Miss.Princess
- Always **Miss.Princess** — never "Miss Princess" (no period = wrong), never "miss.princess" (lowercase = wrong)
- In MVQUEEN-primary copy, Miss.Princess is introduced as: *"our sister brand, Miss.Princess"*
- In Miss.Princess-primary copy, she stands alone — no need to reference MVQUEEN unless brand relationship is the editorial focus
- The two brands are **sister brands**, not sub-brands or lines. Miss.Princess is a peer, not a product category.

---

### Rule 18 — How to Write About the Customer
- She is **she** — never "they" in brand copy (singular they is for accessibility/legal contexts)
- She is never addressed as "girl" in MVQUEEN copy (Miss.Princess may use it sparingly)
- She is never a demographic label (*millennials, Gen Z, women 25–35*) in customer-facing copy
- She is described by her *emotional state and aspirations*, not her age or income

> ❌ "For women aged 25–45 who love fashion."
> ✅ "For the woman who treats getting dressed as a form of self-expression."

---

### Rule 19 — The Test Before You Ship

Read the copy out loud. Then ask:
- Does it sound like a real person who loves this brand wrote this?
- Does it make her feel something before it tells her anything?
- Is there a single word on the Forbidden Words list?
- Is every sentence doing work — or is any sentence just taking up space?

If you can answer yes, yes, no, yes — it ships.
