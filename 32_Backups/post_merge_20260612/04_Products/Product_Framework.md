# 👑 MVQUEEN — Product Framework
### How Products Are Built

---

## What This File Is

This is not the product catalog (what exists). This is not the collection structure (how products are organized). This is the product development framework — how MVQUEEN decides what to build, validates it, develops it, and brings it to market.

Every product that enters the MVQUEEN ecosystem passes through this framework. Products that don't pass don't get built. Products that do get built with the full intention the brand demands.

---

## THE PRODUCT DEVELOPMENT PHILOSOPHY

MVQUEEN does not build products to fill a catalog. It builds products to fill emotional gaps.

The question that precedes every product decision is not *"what can we sell?"* — it is *"what does she need to feel more elevated, more herself, more at home in this world?"*

A product that answers that question with precision earns its place in the ecosystem. A product that doesn't — no matter how commercially logical — doesn't belong here.

---

## PART 1 — THE PRODUCT VALIDATION CRITERIA

Before any product enters development, it must pass all five criteria:

### Criterion 01 — Emotional Necessity
Does this product fill a real emotional gap in the customer's experience?

Not a market gap — an emotional one. There is a difference between "no one makes this" and "this is what she needs to feel how MVQUEEN promises to make her feel."

> ✅ A fragrance that wears close to skin and becomes a signature — fills the emotional gap of wanting to be remembered without trying
> ❌ Another body lotion because the category is large — fills a market gap, not an emotional one

---

### Criterion 02 — Brand World Fit
Does this product belong in the MVQUEEN world — visually, emotionally, and strategically?

A product that requires the brand to communicate differently, design differently, or position differently does not fit. MVQUEEN builds products that extend the world — not ones that require the world to accommodate them.

> ✅ A silk hair wrap — extends the sensory luxury and feminine lifestyle world naturally
> ❌ A protein shake — requires a completely different brand register to sell authentically

---

### Criterion 03 — Quality Delivery
Can this product be delivered at a quality standard consistent with the MVQUEEN pricing tier it will occupy?

Accessible luxury means the experience must match the price point. A product that requires compromise on materials, formulation, or presentation to hit a price point should be repriced — not degraded.

> ✅ A $42 candle in weighted matte packaging with a clean burn formula
> ❌ A $42 candle in cheap packaging with synthetic fragrance — the price creates an expectation the product can't meet

---

### Criterion 04 — Ritual Potential
Can this product become part of a ritual — something she reaches for repeatedly, that becomes part of how she cares for herself?

One-time purchases build revenue. Ritual products build loyalty. MVQUEEN prioritizes products with repeat purchase potential — not because of margin, but because ritual products are the ones that genuinely improve how she feels over time.

> ✅ A daily serum — 60-day supply, becomes part of her morning
> ❌ A single-use sheet mask with no companion products — no ritual anchor

---

### Criterion 05 — Miss.Princess Clarity
If this product could belong to either brand — which one does it actually belong to, and why?

Every product belongs to one brand only. If a product's emotional register, aesthetic, or customer profile could fit either MVQUEEN or Miss.Princess, that ambiguity is resolved at the framework stage — not at launch.

> ✅ A warm, sophisticated eau de parfum — MVQUEEN
> ✅ A sparkly, playful lip gloss in rose gold packaging — Miss.Princess
> ❌ A "feminine" product that's been assigned to MVQUEEN by default — needs reassessment

## PART 2 — THE PRODUCT DEVELOPMENT STAGES

### Stage 01 — Concept
**What happens:** The product idea is defined at its emotional core. Not the formula or the design — the feeling it creates and the gap it fills.

**Deliverables:**
- One-sentence emotional purpose: *"This product exists to make [specific feeling] accessible in [specific context]."*
- Target customer at this specific moment: not the persona broadly, but the specific version of her who needs this
- Brand fit assessment against all five criteria above

**Gate:** All five criteria pass. If any fail, the concept is revised or abandoned.

---

### Stage 02 — Definition
**What happens:** The product is defined specifically — format, formulation direction, key ingredients or materials, size, and price tier.

**Deliverables:**
- Product type and format (serum, candle, midi skirt, etc.)
- Key features or hero ingredient (the one thing that makes it work)
- Price tier (from Pricing_Strategy.md — Tier 1/2/3/4)
- Size/volume/quantity
- Initial naming candidates (from Product_Naming_System.md)

**Gate:** Price tier is confirmed as deliverable at quality standard. If quality requires a higher price than planned — price moves up, not quality down.

---

### Stage 03 — Naming & Positioning
**What happens:** The product is named and its positioning language is locked — before packaging or photography begins.

**Deliverables:**
- Final product name (confirmed against Product_Naming_System.md rules)
- SKU code (from SKU_Systems.md)
- Short positioning statement: one sentence that explains exactly what this product is and for whom
- Collection assignment (if applicable)

**Gate:** Name passes the naming checklist. Positioning statement passes the essence test.

---

### Stage 04 — Packaging & Visual Direction
**What happens:** Packaging is designed and the product's visual identity is defined.

**Deliverables:**
- Packaging materials and format brief (box, bottle, pouch, etc.)
- Color application from color_system.md
- Typography application from typography_system.md
- Photography direction brief for this specific product
- Unboxing experience design (tissue, insert, sticker, etc.)

**Gate:** Packaging delivers the luxury experience at the product's price tier. Does not require the customer to lower her expectations based on price.

---

### Stage 05 — Copy & Content
**What happens:** All customer-facing copy for this product is written before launch.

**Deliverables:**
- Full product description (from Product_Description_Voice.md formula)
- Short product description (30 words for collection pages)
- 3 Instagram captions
- 1 TikTok script
- 1 Pinterest pin title and description
- Email feature copy (for launch email)
- CTA selection from CTA_Library.md

**Gate:** All copy passes the Forbidden Words audit and Voice Consistency Rules check.

---

### Stage 06 — Launch Preparation
**What happens:** The product is prepared for its specific launch context — new drop, seasonal collection, or core catalog addition.

**Deliverables:**
- Launch sequence brief (from Collection_Structure.md launch sequence)
- Inventory confirmed
- Shopify product page built and reviewed
- Email flow trigger set
- Social content scheduled
- Paid ad creative prepared (if applicable — from Ad_Copy_Prompts.md)

**Gate:** Every touchpoint is brand-reviewed before the first customer sees it.

## PART 3 — PRODUCT CATEGORIES & DEVELOPMENT PRIORITIES

### MVQUEEN Core Categories
Each category has its own development rhythm and customer journey:

| Category | Development Lead Time | Ritual Potential | Margin Profile |
|---|---|---|---|
| Skincare | 3–6 months (formulation) | High — daily use | Medium–High |
| Fragrance | 4–8 months (development) | High — signature wear | High |
| Fashion | 2–4 months (production) | Medium — seasonal | Medium |
| Haircare | 3–6 months (formulation) | High — weekly use | Medium |
| Beauty | 2–4 months | Medium–High | Medium |
| Lifestyle / Home | 1–3 months | Medium | Medium |
| Gift Sets | 1–2 months (curation) | Low–Medium | Medium–High |

### Miss.Princess Category Priorities
Miss.Princess prioritizes speed-to-market in trend-adjacent categories:

| Category | Lead Time | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Lip products | 2–3 months | High |
| Hair accessories | 1–2 months | High |
| Nail products | 2–3 months | Medium |
| Mini fragrances | 3–4 months | High |
| Skincare (playful) | 3–5 months | Medium |

## PART 4 — PRODUCT RETIREMENT

Products leave the catalog when:

1. **Quality can no longer be maintained** at the price point — raise the price or retire
2. **The product no longer fits the brand world** — a category the brand has evolved away from
3. **Sales performance is consistently below threshold** for 2+ quarters with no strategic reason to maintain
4. **A better version replaces it** — the new product makes the old one redundant

**Retirement protocol:**
- Existing inventory sold through (no clearance sale language — "final availability")
- Product page archived, not deleted (SEO value preserved)
- Customer notification for high-loyalty products (top repurchasers notified directly)
- Replacement product communicated if one exists
